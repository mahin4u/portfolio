I moved from Dhaka to Mechelen to study International Tourism & Leisure. By my
second year I had quietly pivoted into **Global Supply Chain** — and in doing
so, I realized I'd been living my own case study the whole time.

## Every journey is a logistics problem

Relocating across the world is, functionally, a supply chain:

1. **Sourcing** — where do you come from, and what do you carry?
2. **Transit** — the friction of borders, languages, paperwork.
3. **Last mile** — actually *arriving*, and integrating.

The goods are you. The constraints are real. And the lesson is the same one
that runs through everything I do: **optimize the whole system, not the
individual step.**

## Blink Media was a supply chain too

When I founded [Blink Media](/story) in Mechelen, I thought I was starting a
branding studio. What I was really doing was building an operations engine —
turning creative input into consistent, repeatable output for local
restaurants.

- Raw material: a restaurant's story.
- Process: branding, content, systems.
- Delivery: a business that runs smoother than it did yesterday.

## Together is better

The thread through all of it — supply chain, media, trading, photography — is
the belief that **connected systems beat isolated brilliance.**

Make the world a little bigger. Make it a little more beautiful. Make sure
everyone still has a right to smile. That's the only KPI that really matters.
